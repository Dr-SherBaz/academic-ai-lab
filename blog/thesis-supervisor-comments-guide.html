How to Organise Supervisor Comments for Faster Thesis Revision

May 1, 2026

Why supervisor comments feel chaotic

Supervisors review your work holistically, but their comments rarely arrive in

a neatly ordered sequence. One paragraph mentions referencing style, the next

jumps to theoretical framework, and a margin note flags a missing data table.

Without a system, you risk missing subtle but critical observations.

Categorise every comment

Before you start revising, classify each comment into one of these categories:

  • Structural — chapter organisation, section flow, argument logic
  • Methodological — research design, sampling, analysis approach
  • Content — depth of discussion, literature integration, gap analysis
  • Formatting — citations, headings, tables, figures, pagination
  • Language — clarity, academic tone, grammar, terminology

Create a response matrix

For each comment, record:

  • The original comment text
  • Your proposed action
  • The section(s) affected
  • Whether it requires additional data or reading
  • Priority (must-fix / should-address / optional)

This matrix becomes your revision roadmap. It also serves as evidence when

you submit the revised draft — showing that every comment received a thoughtful

response.

Prioritise by dependency

Some changes cascade. Fixing a theoretical framework might change your

literature review. Moving a section might affect cross-referencing. Always

start with structural and methodological comments before addressing formatting

and language.

When to ask for clarification

If a supervisor comment is genuinely unclear, prepare a specific question:

  • "You noted the methodology needs strengthening — would a comparative case

study approach address this?"

  • "Regarding the comment on Chapter 3 — are you concerned about the scope of

the literature, or the critical engagement with it?"

Specific questions show you have engaged with the feedback and are ready to act.
